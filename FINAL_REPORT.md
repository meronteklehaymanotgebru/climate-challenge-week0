## Executive Summary

This analysis examined climate data from five African nations (Ethiopia, Kenya, 
Sudan, Tanzania, Nigeria) using NASA POWER satellite data (2015-2026).

### Key Findings

1. Temperature decreases by ~0.65 C per 100m elevation
2. Sudan faces extreme heat on 66% of days
3. Ethiopia's highland climate is a natural advantage but vulnerable to warming
4. Coastal cities face compound flood risks from intense rainfall
5. Statistical tests confirm climate differences are significant (p < 0.001)

### COP32 Recommendations

1. Prioritize Sudan for heat adaptation finance
2. Protect Ethiopian highland agriculture through climate-smart practices
3. Invest in flood infrastructure for Lagos and Dar es Salaam
4. Establish regional early warning systems for drought and floods
